Plan and Implement Inventory: Steps

This topic briefly outlines the steps involved in the inventory stage. It will be helpful to refer to these steps when implementing ROOFER for the first time.

 

Your ROOFER inventory serves as the foundation for all that you will do with the ROOFER application. Therefore, it pays to make sure that your inventory process is well planned and thorough.

Step 1: Plan the Inventory Tree

Scope and Structure of the Inventory Tree

The first step in the inventory process, if you are implementing ROOFER for the first time, is to determine the scope and structure of the information tree that describes the physical inventory you manage. The section Plan the Inventory Tree provides some guidance for this step.

Hierarchical Levels

In order to either plan or manage the tree effectively, you should first be familiar with the hierarchical levels used for storing inventory data; these levels are described in Understanding ROOFER Inventory Hierarchy

Step 4: Create Records

Transferring Records from Existing Software

It's possible that inventory data may already exist in an asset management program. In this case, contact your ROOFER support representative for advice on how to transfer records int ROOFER .

Creating New Records

The records for the upper levels of the inventory tree (Organization and Site) need to be created by someone with the Master Planner A user with Master Planner Role has all the rights of a Work Planners, and can also generate multi-year work plan scenarios, and edit any and all data saved by other users of the program. Master Planner is the highest level of user privileges. level of permissions. After these upper-level records are created, they may be edited by users with lower permissions Roles.

After the upper levels of the inventory tree have been established and entered, the next step is to create Building records for the Buildings at your Site or one of your Sites. A Building record includes critical information such as how the building or facility is used, and its size, age, and number of floors.

Note: Only after at least one of its constituent Buildings has been created may a Complex be created, if desired.

After you have created a Building record in the ROOFER inventory, you will be able to add records that describe the roof sections. Inspections are done on the sections; then ROOFER applies its condition assessment algorithms to calculate condition indexes for the sections and prioritizes needed replacements and/or repairs for work planning.

Step 5: Collect Inventory Data

Next comes the process of collecting detailed information about your roofing inventory. This effort may be done by existing staff, or may be contracted out.

The process of putting this data into ROOFER will probably be your single most intensive effort in working with the program, but for the most part it this a one-time effort.

Note: If both BUILDER and ROOFER are being used, the upper inventory levels are common to both tools. However, the data pertaining to inventory inhabiting these levels is not currently shared between the two software applications.
