About Condition Assessments

In ROOFERâ„¢, condition assessments are performed at the individual roof section level. Two types of inspections are available for a roof section:

  • Visual inspection
  • Insulation inspection.

A visual inspection consists of a distress survey of the roof section and its sub-components. The insulation inspection is a core sample inspection of the roof section's insulation. Both types of inspections are entered at the Inspections tab for a selected roof section.

For details about entering an inspection, start at Performing Roof Condition Assessments.

Inspection Overview

When performing a visual inspection distress survey, the inspector records information about a roof section by recording the distresses on each sub-component of the roof section. Each distress has associated defects that are given a severity rating (low, medium, or high) and density range (quantity) based on established criteria. ROOFER uses this inspection information to calculate the condition indexes appropriate to the type of roof section being inspected. These indexes are combined to calculate the roof section's Roof Condition Index (RCI Roof Condition Index). The RCI Roof Condition Index is then tied to Maintenance and Rehabilitation (M&R Maintenance and Rehabilitation) costs to form the basis for M&R Maintenance and Rehabilitation planning and budgeting.

Membrane Roof Condition Indexes

ROOFER uses the inspection to calculate the Membrane Condition Index (MCI Membrane Condition Index) and the Flashing Condition Index (FCI) for a membrane roof section. The MCI Membrane Condition Index and FCI are then combined with the Insulation Condition Index (ICI Insulation Condition Index) from an insulation inspection to generate the roof section's RCI Roof Condition Index.

Metal Panel Roof Condition Indexes

ROOFER uses the inspection to calculate the Panel Condition Index (PCI) and the Flashing Condition Index (FCI) for a metal panel roof section. The PCI and FCI are then combined with the Insulation Condition Index (ICI Insulation Condition Index) from an insulation inspection to generate the roof section's RCI Roof Condition Index.

Steep Roof (Non-Metal) Roof Condition Indexes

ROOFER uses the inspection to calculate the Substrate Condition Index (SCI System Condition Index - a condition rating for a Building System. For each System, the SCI is computed by taking the average of its Components' CIs, weighted by replacement cost.) and the Flashing Condition Index (FCI) for a non-metal steep roof section. The SCI System Condition Index - a condition rating for a Building System. For each System, the SCI is computed by taking the average of its Components' CIs, weighted by replacement cost. and FCI are then combined with the Insulation Condition Index (ICI Insulation Condition Index) from an insulation inspection to generate the roof section's RCI Roof Condition Index.

Distress Survey Condition Index (CI) Computation

The Distress Survey Process

The distress survey process is illustrated by the diagram shown below.

For distress surveys, the combination of distress type, severity level, and density of the sub-components of the roof section are used to determine the deduct value for the individual sub-components. The deduct values for each combination of distress, severity, and density were determined during ERDC-CERL U.S. Army Engineer Research and Development Center, Construction Engineering Research Laboratory's research effort using rating values from a team of experts.

Correction Factor for Multiple Distresses

It should be noted that if more than one distress exists, the sum of the deduct values for the distresses present must be corrected. This is because as additional distress types and/or severity levels occur on the sub-component, the impact of a given distress on the condition rating becomes less. This correction correlates the expert panel's ratings and the computed indexes. The correction factor is a function of the summed total of the individual deduct values, a minimum individual deduct value, and the number of different distress types and severity level combinations found. The correction factor values were developed from the rating panel data and are applied directly to the deduct value to produce a corrected deduct value.

Computing the RCI

After all the sub-component condition indexes are determined for a roof section, the RCI Roof Condition Index is computed based on the individual sub-component CIs and their weight factors. If sampling is used, the sample unit indexes are aggregated by computing the average of the representative samples weighted by size. All calculations are made at the time an inspection is entered.

Condition Index Rollups

The preceding sections described how a condition index is computed for a roof section after an inspection. The inspection CIs form the basis for computing a current RCI Roof Condition Index for all roof sections in the hierarchy. Individual roof section RCIs are then rolled-up hierarchically to the overall roof condition indexes for Buildings: The Building RCIs are rolled-up into Complex, Site and Organization RCIs. Collectively, trends can be monitored, and M&R Maintenance and Rehabilitation can be scheduled accordingly.
