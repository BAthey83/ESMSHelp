Understanding ROOFER Inventory Hierarchy

In the SMS Sustainment Management System, a series of hierarchical levels allows information such as condition assessment results to be communicated upwards, aggregating the information as it is incorporated into progressively higher levels of the inventory tree.

Note: Lower hierarchical levels in individual modules (BUILDER, ROOFER, etc.) of the SMS Sustainment Management System are designed and named for best fit with the module of which they are a part. The levels named "Organization" and "Site", however, remain constant throughout the SMS Sustainment Management System.

The ROOFER™ Inventory Hierarchy

The levels of hierarchy in ROOFER are as follows:

  •               Organization (optional)
  •              Site
  •               Complex
  •               Facility / Building
  • , ,    Roof Section

Each of these levels is described briefly under headings below.

The hierarchy that has been designed and built out in a given ROOFER implementation is shown in the navigation pane (see illustration below).

To navigate through the tree, activate a "plus" box to expand the inventory tree another level. Activating a "minus" box rolls the tree view back to that level.

Hierarchy Tree Requirements

The top level of the hierarchy tree:

  1. Must be either an Organization or a Site;
  2. May contain only a single item; there will be no other Organization or Site at that level of the tree.

Organization

Organization is the highest possible level in the ROOFER inventory tree. However, it is not a required level. Depending on the scope of your ROOFER implementation, it is possible to start with the Site level instead.

Unlike the other hierarchical levels, the Organization level may be nested more than one deep. When designing the inventory tree before implementing and filling out your inventory, it is important to accommodate in the inventory tree the highest possible Organization level you may want to expand to in the future. For more detail, see Plan the Inventory Tree.

In the hierarchy tree, an Organization can have any combination of Sites and/or Organizations beneath it.

For more information about this hierarchical level, see About Organizations.

Site

The level below Organization is the Site, which usually refers to a geographic location. The Area Cost Factor A multiplier that adjusts national average labor/material/equipment costs to better reflect local costs. For example, in a remote area with high shipping costs and a tight labor market, the Area Cost Factor might be 1.3. The Area Cost Factor is generally a number between 0.8 and 1.2 for continental U.S. locations, but it can be even greater than 2.0 in high-cost geographic locations. is one of the Location Factors that is set at the Site level. Other features that are set at the Site level are:

  • Seismic Zone
  • HVAC Zone

(In practice, a Site as a whole will typically have a facility manager who is responsible for day-to-day management of the Site, often with the assistance of subsidiary personnel.)

Just as an Organization can have one or more Sites and/or other Organizations created underneath it, in the SMS Sustainment Management System hierarchy a Site can have any combination of Complexes and/or Buildings beneath it.

For more information, see About Sites.

Complex

A Complex is an optional grouping of Buildings or facilities. This feature is particularly useful for groupings of Buildings with similar Building Use types or locations.

Note: Grouping Buildings into a Complex is done after creating the Buildings, not before.

For more information, see About Complexes.

Facility / Building

A Building is a stand-alone building or other facility. At this level in the hierarchy, you can see details such as area and year constructed, roof assessment history for the Building as a whole, and work item history. Attachments such as floor plans and photographs can be associated with the Building level.

At the Building level on up, only the RCI Roof Condition Index is displayed. Sub-indices to the RCI Roof Condition Index can be viewed at the roof section level.

For more information, see About Buildings.

Roof Section

A Building can have one or more roof sections. Where parts of a Building's roof are different in material, style, or age, it is good to separate each of these parts into its own roof section.

At the roof section level in the ROOFER inventory hierarchy is where the constituent components of the RCI Roof Condition Index are shown for the types of roof available in ROOFER:

Membrane

  • MCI Membrane Condition Index - the Membrane Condition Index
  • FCI - the Flashing Condition Index
  • ICI Insulation Condition Index - the Insulation Condition Index.

Metal Panel

  • PCI - the Panel Condition Index
  • FCI - the Flashing Condition Index
  • ICI Insulation Condition Index - the Insulation Condition Index.

Steep Roof: Non-Metal

  • SCI System Condition Index - a condition rating for a Building System. For each System, the SCI is computed by taking the average of its Components' CIs, weighted by replacement cost. - the Substrate Condition Index
  • FCI - the Flashing Condition Index
  • ICI Insulation Condition Index - the Insulation Condition Index.

Note: In ROOFER, the abbreviation FCI stands for Flashing Condition Index. In the BUILDER SMS Sustainment Management System module, FCI stands for Facility Condition Index.

For more information, see About Roof Sections.
