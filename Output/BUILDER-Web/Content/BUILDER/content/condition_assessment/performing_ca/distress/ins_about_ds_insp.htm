About Distress Surveys

This topic introduces the distress survey type of condition assessment, in which the inspector lists the distresses in a Component-Section. Beginning with BUILDER Version 3.4, however, a distress survey condition assessment also allows the inspector to select, on a subcomponent by subcomponent basis, between (a) direct rating and (b) entering descriptions of distresses found.

The topic also supplies links to distress survey tasks.

BUILDER 3.4 Changes to Distress Surveys

The distress survey interface has been changed, introducing the ability to assign direct ratings at the subcomponent level as an alternative to listing distresses for the subcomponent.

New Feature: Direct Rating Option for Subcomponents

A new column in the distress survey table of subcomponents (the table you see when you select Distress Survey as your inspection type) allows you the option to apply a direct rating to individual subcomponents as an alternative to listing distresses for them.

By contrast, when you select Direct Rating as the inspection type to perform, that direct rating applies to the whole Component-Section.

Interface Changes

At the table of subcomponents that is presented when you select Distress Survey as the inspection type, the following changes have been made from Version 3.3.12:

  • D/F column deleted. The subcomponent-level D/F (Defect Free) checkbox column has been removed. Instead, you have the option to select G+ in the Direct Rating column.
  • Insp. column deleted. The Insp. (Inspected) checkbox column has been removed.
  • N/A column moved. The N/A checkbox column has been moved before the Subcomponent column.
  • Direct Rating column added. For any given subcomponent, you can select a direct rating from the dropdown list in the Direct Rating column as an alternative to linking to the "Distresses" popup and listing distresses there. Subject to your organization's policies, technically at least you can mix distress listings and direct ratings at the subcomponent level.

Note: If both a direct rating and distresses are entered for the same subcomponent, the direct rating will take precedence.

What is a Distress Survey?

A distress survey is the most thorough type of condition assessment that can be performed on a Component-Section level in BUILDER. It is the Component-Section level condition assessment type that provides the greatest detail as to a Section's deficiencies. It is also more time consuming to perform than a direct rating.

Once details as to the severity and density of a Section's deficiencies have been entered, BUILDER will calculate the appropriate condition rating for the Section. After Sections have been inspected, the condition of the Sections are rolled up to indicate overall condition of Components and Systems, and so on up the asset tree.

When to Use a Distress Survey

To read engineering advice concerning the best timing for a distress survey, see About Choosing Condition Assessment Type.

Written for BUILDER Version 3, this document is available by download at https://support.sms.erdc.dren.mil/downloads/erdc-cerl-technical-publications.
