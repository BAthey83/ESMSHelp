Distress Survey: Distress-Level Data

This topic provides detail about how to populate or edit distress details in a distress survey that does not use sampling.

WARNING: After you enter or edit a distress, if you don't activate the blue checkmark at the left side of the distress record to be saved, your work on that record will be lost!

The distress survey data screen and the "Distresses" popup window provide storage for a wealth of information pertaining to a distress survey inspection of a Component-Section. This topic focuses on working in the "Distresses" popup window. For information at the inspection and subcomponent level rather than the distress level, see the topic Distress Survey Data (Not Sampling).

When the Distress Survey radio button is selected under the Type of condition assessment, the content area of the BUILDER screen will resemble the screenshot below.

This screenshot shows how a distress survey will appear when first created. By default, all subcomponents are marked N/A for "Not Applicable" at the outset. As you enter inspection data, you will either be entering distresses for subcomponents that are present, or assigning a direct rating (see Using Direct Ratings within a Distress Survey in Distress Survey Data (Not Sampling)). This topic details how to view, add, edit, and delete distresses.

View Subcomponent Distress Details

The "Distresses" popup window, shown below, is where you can view, add, copy, edit, or delete individual distresses against a subcomponent. Access to this popup window is via the Distresses link in the Distresses column of the distress survey screen.

The header section of the popup identifies the Component, Section, and subcomponent, and shows the unit of measure (UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet)) being used to measure the subcomponent quantity and distress quantity. The (limited) option to change the unit of measure is discussed in Change Subcomponent/Distress Unit of Measure .

The lower section of the popup window is where distress details are recorded. Descriptions of these details are listed in Add a Distress. Subsequent documentation segments will instruct how to add, edit, or delete a distress.

Change Subcomponent/Distress Unit of Measure

BUILDER users who enter distresses have the option to change the unit of measure from a non-"each" default selected by BUILDER to a unit of measure of "each." If the Alternative UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) "Each" checkbox is marked in the "Distresses" popup window, this means that the unit of measure used for the component will be Each (EA) rather than a differing default UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) such as linear feet (LF).

The checkbox for selecting an alternative UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) of "Each" is the only user-editable field in the upper section of the "Distresses" popup.

Add a Distress

There are two alternative ways to initiate adding a distress in the "Distresses" popup:

  1. Activate the Add new record button above the table of distresses, and create a distress from scratch, or
  2. Activate the Copy [Distress] button on the toolbar to use an existing distress listed in the popup as the starting point for a new distress.

For how to use the second method listed above, see Copy a Distress.

List of Distress Details

This segment will show how to enter the following distress details:

  • Distress Type (Required). This is in the column labeled Distress. From the dropdown list, select a type of distress present on the subcomponent. For definitions of all the distresses, see Appendix A in the Condition Assessment Reference Manual* or the Online Help documentation.
  • Severity(Required). Select the severity of the distress from the dropdown list. The applicable severities for each distress type are listed and defined in Appendix A in the Condition Assessment Reference Manual* and the Online Help documentation.

    Tip: You might find that you have two distresses of the same type, severity, and density. Such a pair of distresses should be combined into one row with an increased density value.

  • Subcomponent Quantity. Enter the subcomponent quantity in the unit of measure listed above the grid. When the subcomponent's unit of measure should be"Each" but the unit of measure listed in the Subcomponent UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) field is not, mark the Alternative UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) "Each" checkbox and then enter the number of subcomponents.
  • Distress Quantity. Enter the quantity of the subcomponent that has the distress in the unit of measure listed above the grid. When the subcomponent's unit of measure should be "Each" but the unit of measure listed in the Subcomponent UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) field is not, mark the Alternative UM Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; English; Metric.Unit of Measure. Examples: Each; LF (linear feet) "Each" checkbox and then enter the number of subcomponents with the distress.
  • Density(Required). Select the density of the distress from the dropdown list. The formulas used to compute the density for each distress type are listed in the definitions of the distresses in Appendix A in the Condition Assessment Reference Manual* and the Online Help documentation.
  • "Critical" Checkbox. Mark this checkbox if the distress on the subcomponent is critical. Marking this checkbox will make the work item created for this Section "Must Do."
  • Emergency Service Call ("ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call") Checkbox. Mark this checkbox if the distress on the subcomponent requires an ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call.
  • ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call Number. If an ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call was issued to respond to this distress, enter its number for your records in this field.
  • ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call Date. If an ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call was issued to respond to this distress and has been completed, enter the date of completion in this field. Distresses with completed service calls are assumed to have no effect on the subcomponent condition.

Instructions with Screenshots

To add a distress against the subcomponent shown in the header section of the "Distresses" popup,

  1. Activate the + Add new record button at the top of the table of distresses.

  2. Select a distress from the dropdown list of options in the Distress column.

  3. Activate the Help link in the rightmost column to review the specifications page for the selected distress. A sample specifications page is shown below.

    Tip: Reviewing the specifications page is a good idea to do before selecting a severity for the distress because for some distresses, not all severity levels (Low, Medium, High) will be legitimate entries. For example, for the distress "Electrical Ground Inadequate or Unintentional" (listed as "Elec Grnd Inad or Uninten" in the "Distress" dropdown list), only the severity level High is a legitimate entry, as shown on the specifications page.

    Note: All specification pages are documented in Condition Assessment Reference Manual appendixes, and listed in the BUILDER Online Help documentation.

  4. Select the appropriate severity level from the dropdown list of options in the Severity column, keeping in mind any limitation in the Low/Medium/High options indicated by the specifications page for that distress.
  5. Enter subcomponent quantity and distress quantity as appropriate.
  6. Select the appropriate density range from the dropdown list of options in the Density column. You can activate the Help link in the rightmost column to review the appropriate matching between condition of the subcomponent and the corresponding density level to be selected.
  7. Marking the Critical checkbox will trigger a repair work item within the first year of a work plan even if there is still a remaining maintenance life (RML Remaining Maintenance LifeRemaining Maintenance Life) for the Component-Section.
  8. ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call stands for Emergency Service Call. If an Emergency Service Call was generated due to the distress, mark this checkbox and fill in the number and date .

    Note:Critical and ESC Emergency Service CallsEmergency Service Call should not be used together.

  9. Save as described in Save Distress Data.

Copy a Distress

To copy a distress from within the same subcomponent to use as a starting place for a new distress,

  1. Activate the Copy button on the "Distresses" popup window toolbar. The "Copy Distresses" popup window will appear, providing checkboxes in the first column for selecting distresses.

  2. Mark the checkbox(es) of the distress(es) you wish to copy. Shown below is a sample selection.

  3. If you wish to copy all of the distresses shown, mark the checkbox in the first column of the header bar of the distress table.

  4. Copy or Cancel: After you have made your selection(s), activate the  Copy button on the toolbar to complete the copy/copies. Alternatively, activate the  Cancel button to cancel the operation and return to the "Distresses" window.

 

Edit a Distress

To edit a distress that has been entered against the subcomponent shown in the header section of the "Distresses" popup window,

  1. Activate the pencil icon shown in the left column, as shown above. The display will change, as shown below:

  2. Edit the desired fields. (Remember that you can consult the specifications page for the distress by activating the Help link in the rightmost column).
  3. Save as described in Save Distress Data.

Save Distress Data

To save distress data, you will need to first confirm your edits in each table row as follows:

  1. To confirm the distress, activate the blue checkmark icon on the left in that row of data. (Alternatively, if you wish to cancel the changes, activate the cancellation icon; it looks like a "Do Not Enter" sign.)

    After confirming the individual distress, you will be returned to the view showing a pencil icon in the leftmost column.

    WARNING: If your BUILDER instance times out, you may lose work that is not yet fully saved.

  2. At this point, if you wish to add, edit, or delete other distress records in the "Distresses" popup window, you may do so before closing the popup.
  3. Activate Close on the toolbar when you are finished adding distresses to that subcomponent. You will be returned to the Subcomponent table, and you should see that the number of distress has been updated.

Delete a Distress

To delete a distress listed in the "Distresses" popup window,

  1. In the table of distresses, activate the deletion button in the second column of the record you wish to delete.

  2. Confirm the deletion in the popup confirmation box. (Alternatively, you can select the Cancel button to cancel the deletion.)

  3. Activate  Save on the content area toolbar. You will be returned to the Inspection tab showing the table of subcomponents.
