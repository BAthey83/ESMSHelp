Appendix D: Shingle Roofing (SR) Distress Survey Definitions

General Notes

  • These definitions apply to shingled roofing (SR Shingle RoofingShingle Roofing) surfaces and flashing components.
  • Where multiple severity levels are present for a given distress, record each separately.
  • Distress quantities or distress density may be recorded. If distress quantities are recorded, density ranges will be computed in BUILDER/BRED BUILDER Remote Entry Database (BuilderRED)BUILDER Remote Entry Database (BuilderRED). If distress densities are recorded, distress quantities will be blank in BUILDER/BRED BUILDER Remote Entry Database (BuilderRED)BUILDER Remote Entry Database (BuilderRED).
  • If during the course of the inspection additional occurrences are found of distress-severity combinations, adjust the quantity or density as necessary.
  • To estimate density when distress quantities are not recorded, follow the density definitions for the individual distresses. However, for use in BUILDER, density may be estimated since density ranges are used, not the precise density value. Generalized visual cues are offered below in Table D-1, but these cues may not be applicable for certain distresses.
  • The distress definitions that follow are reproduced from ROOFER: Steep Roofing Inventory Procedures and Inspection and Distress Manual for Asphalt Shingle Roofs (Bailey 1999), CERL Technical Report 99/100. This report can be downloaded from the ROOFER section of the SMS Sustainment Management SystemSustainment Management System support site at https://support.sms.erdc.dren.mil/downloads/roofer.
