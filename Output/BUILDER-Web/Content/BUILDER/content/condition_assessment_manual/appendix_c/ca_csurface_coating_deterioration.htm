Single-Ply Roofing: Surface Coating Deterioration

Definition:

Surface coating deterioration includes wear, blistering, or peeling of any surface coating applied for fire protection (such as adhesive coating and sand on an EPDM membrane) or solar reflectivity, but not waterproofing.

Severity Levels:

Low - Color of underlying membrane can be seen through the coating or membrane has lost protection (for membrane with coating protection that does not have sand or mineral matter embedded).

Medium - Membrane area has lost the sand or mineral matter portion of the coating protection (for membrane with coating protection that has sand or mineral matter embedded).

Measurement:

  • Measure the square feet of each affected area and rate at the highest severity level present.
  • When large quantities of this problem are present, the representative sampling technique may be used.

Density:

x 100 = Problem Density

A = total area of surface coating deterioration (ft)

B = total area of roof section being rated.
