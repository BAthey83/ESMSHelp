Single-Ply Roofing: Ridges

Definition:

Ridges are long, narrow (usually less than 3 in.), raised portions of the roof membrane. Usually ridges occur directly above the insulation board joints.

Severity Levels:

Low - All ridges are rated low severity as a minimum.

High - Open breaks have developed in the ridge allowing water to penetrate.

Measurement:

Measure length of ridges running in all directions. When many ridges are present, the representative sampling technique may be used.

Density:

x 100 = Problem Density

A = total length of membrane ridges (ft).

B = total area or roof section being rated (sq ft).
