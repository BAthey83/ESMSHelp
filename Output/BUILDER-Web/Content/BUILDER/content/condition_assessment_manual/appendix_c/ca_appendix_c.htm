APPENDIX C: Single Ply Roofing Distress Survey Definitions

General Notes

  1. These definitions apply to single-ply (SP Single PlySingle Ply) roofing surfaces and flashing components.
  2. Where multiple severity levels are present for a given distress, record each separately.
  3. Distress quantities or distress density may be recorded. If distress quantities are recorded, density ranges will be computed in BUILDER/BuilderRED. If distress densities are recorded, distress quantities will be blank in BUILDER/BuilderRED.
  4. If during the course of the inspection additional occurrences are found of distress-severity combinations, adjust the quantity or density as necessary.
  5. To estimate density when distress quantities are not recorded, follow the density definitions for the individual distresses. However, for use in BUILDER, density may be estimated since density ranges are used, not the precise density value. Generalized visual cues are offered below in Table C-1, but they may not be applicable for certain distresses.
  6.
