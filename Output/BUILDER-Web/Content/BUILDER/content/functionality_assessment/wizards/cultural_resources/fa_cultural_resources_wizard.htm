Cultural Resources Wizard

Note: The Cultural Resources Wizard is available at the Building/Facility level and at the Functional Area level.

To access the Cultural Resources Wizard, open an existing Cultural Resources assessment, or else start a new one as follows:

  1. Activate the Functionality ribbon in the Navigation pane.
  2. Navigate to the desired Building/Facility or Functional Area.
  3. Verify that you are at the Assessments tab in the content area of the screen.
  4. Activate the button. The "New Assessment" popup will appear.
  5. Select the Cultural Resources assessment set by activating the radio button next to Cultural Resources.
  6. Activate the button at the bottom of the popup. The header Cultural Resources should appear on the first line in the table in the content area. If it doesn't, it may be that BUILDER timed out; try logging out and back in, and repeating the steps to this point.
  7. Once your new assessment is created, activate the Assessment Wizard button in the Response column of the second line of the table. The "Assessment Wizard" popup should appear.


The Cultural Resources Wizard will appear in a popup:


The wizard will have a series of questions that help determine the Building's Cultural Resources status. If you are unsure of the question, you can activate the arrow to the left of the question to get a further explanation of the question.

If you answered Yes to any of the first set of numbered questions, then the building is historically significant and you must fill out the following information.

  • Classification. Select the historic classification of the Building from the dropdown list.
  • Building Style. Select the building style from the dropdown list.
  • HABS Historic American Buildings Survey / HAER Historic American Engineering Record. The Historic American Buildings Survey ( HABS Historic American Buildings Survey) and Historic American Engineering Record ( HAER Historic American Engineering Record) are the national historical architectural and engineering documentation programs of the National Park Service that promote documentation incorporated into the HABS Historic American Buildings Survey/ HAER Historic American Engineering Record collections in the Library of Congress. If HABS Historic American Buildings Survey/ HAER Historic American Engineering Record documentation has been submitted for this building, note identification/registration numbers here.
  • Register Number. If the building is listed on the National Register, enter its register number.

To record the results of the wizard, activate the Save button at the upper left of the content area. When finished, activate Save, then Close.
