The Enterprise version of the Sustainment Management System (SMS) is the newest version under development by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers at its Engineering Research and Development Center–Construction Engineering Research Laboratory (ERDC-CERL) in Champaign, IL.

In contrast to legacy versions of the Sustainment Management System, which continue to be offered side by side with Enterprise SMS, the Enterprise version combines into one software program the information and capabilities formerly contained in separate products.

Users can import and access real property information from multiple domains formerly isolated from each other in separate products such as BUILDER™, RAILER™, and ROOFER™. This information can be utilized one domain at a time, or information from two or more domains can be viewed simultaneously.

    Do I Need Enterprise SMS Web, the Enterprise SMS Field Application, or Both?

    Note: The Enterprise SMS field application offers two modes, Desktop and Tablet. Tablet functionality is restricted to performing assessment plans and navigating asset information.

    Work Flow

    The typical work flow interaction between Enterprise SMS Web and the Enterprise SMS field application typically follows these steps:

    1. Inventory and assessment plans are entered into Enterprise SMS Desktop. assessment plans may alternatively be entered in the field using Enterprise SMS Tablet.
    2. Synchronization is performed between Enterprise SMS Desktop and Enterprise SMS Web.
    3. Work items are generated in Enterprise SMS Web, in the Work task area, by specifying threshold levels for condition index and remaining service life.

    Integration Between Enterprise SMS Web, Desktop, and Tablet

    As shown in the diagram below, Enterprise SMS Web, Enterprise SMS Desktop, and (if applicable) Enterprise SMS Tablet are each connected to a central server. In the case of Enterprise SMS Web, synchronization of data to and from the server occurs automatically. With Enterprise SMS Desktop and Enterprise SMS Tablet, however, you must initiate a synchronization to (a) upload information (such as inventory that has been entered, or data on assessment plans that have been performed), or to (b) download Workflow specifications or the latest information from Enterprise SMS Web.
