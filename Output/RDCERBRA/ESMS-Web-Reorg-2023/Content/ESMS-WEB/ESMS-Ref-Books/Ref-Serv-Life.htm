Service Life Books

Data regarding the service life expectancy, in years, of all types of Asset Components are built into Enterprise SMS. The data Enterprise SMS uses in contained in one or more Service Life books.

Accessing Service Life Books

To access Service Life books,

  1. Open the Reference Books drop-down menu.
  2. Select the Service Life button, as highlighted above.

Now, you can select a Service Life book.

About Service Life Books

Service Life books contain life expectancy data for all Component types available within your Organization. Each Component will have an expected Service Life and an expected paint life associated with it through the Service Life book. A default Service Life book is provided for each Organization. One or more custom Service Life books can also be created.

The default Service Life books are based on years of research, analyzing many different assets over their lifecycle. These data points are only approximations, so a custom Service Life book can help produce more accurate work generation for Components in your Inventory that do not follow the predicted Service Life.

Service Life data is used to calculate the CI Condition Index of a Component using a mathematical model that approximates a Component's condition on a 1-100 scale. This calculation is based on how long the Component has been in service and how long the Component is expected to be in service. Modifying the expected Service Life of an asset can increase or decrease the Condition of the asset within Enterprise SMS, depending on its current Condition.

Service Life Data

The Service Life data used by Enterprise SMS is contained in one or more Service Life books. Multiple Service Life books can be associated with different Organizations within Enterprise SMS, as needed.

Default Service Life Book

The default Service Life book provided with Enterprise SMS and it is associated with the top level of the Organization. The scope of its data, therefore, is the entire Organization.

You can choose to depend exclusively on the data in the default Service Life book, or you can create one or more custom Service Life books.

How ESMS Gathers Service Life Data

The following is an example of how Enterprise SMS gathers Service Life data.

Imagine a heavy-graded carpet placed in an area with moderate foot traffic. The carpet has a 20-year life expectancy. The life expectancy data is an average. The final average is determined more by the specialized expertise of multiple facility managers than by statistically precise field data.

Gathering statistically precise data is virtually impossible — even for the carpet's manufacturer. As for the Enterprise SMS database, it must match service life data with a generic Component type to create a final, useable average.

The expected Service Life is a key factor that determines two important measures of how well a particular Component is performing. The two measurements are its current condition index and its remaining Service Life.

Service Life is used to determine an expected deterioration rate in estimating the current condition in the absence of sequential condition assessment data. It is also used in the models that compute the remaining Service Life on the basis of current CI Condition Index and the rate of deterioration.

Source of Service Life Data

The Service Life data in Enterprise SMS is derived from data collected at ERDC-CERL U.S. Army Engineer Research and Development Center, Construction Engineering Research Laboratory over many years of research. This research includes the former BUILDER web resource project as well as an earlier study that resulted in the Maintenance Resource Prediction Model (MRPM). The MRPM has served as the foundation for a number of facility maintenance models.

Your experience, however, may vary in terms of how long items last in your Assets. Enterprise SMS lets you edit the service life data to reflect your knowledge and experience with the various Asset Components you manage.
